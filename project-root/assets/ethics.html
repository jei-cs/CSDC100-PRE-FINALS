Ethical Use of Generative AI

November 24, 2025

What is Ethics in AI and why is it important?

AI tools are only as reliable as the data they’re trained on — and the people who build them (Lparsons, 2025). There are a lot of issues surrounding the use of AI technology in different fields today. That is why it is with utmost importance that we should discuss and learn about the proper and ethical use of AI. Ethics is a set of moral principles which help us discern between right and wrong. AI ethics is a multidisciplinary field that studies how to optimize the beneficial impact of artificial intelligence (AI) while reducing risks and adverse outcomes (Ibm, 2025).

1. Responsible use in academe

AI use in academic settings runs the risk of promoting dishonesty and undermining the learning process. Students must use AI tools as supplements to their own work, ensuring they understand the material and can critically evaluate AI-generated content. Proper citation of AI assistance is also essential to maintain academic integrity. Misuse of AI in academics can lead to students' over-reliance on technology, hindering their critical thinking and problem-solving skills development. AI should be used to enhance learning, not replace it. Policies like honor codes and guidelines can help set clear expectations for ethical AI use in educational contexts. Further more, teaching students about the ethical implications of AI can foster a culture of responsibility and integrity.

2. Avoiding plagiarism and misuse

AI tools can generate content that may be easily misrepresented as original work. It is crucial to avoid plagiarism by properly attributing AI-generated content and ensuring transparency about its use. Misuse of AI, such as generating misleading information or deepfakes, can have serious ethical implications. Users must be vigilant about the accuracy and authenticity of AI outputs, verifying information before dissemination. Establishing clear guidelines on acceptable AI use can help prevent unethical practices and promote responsible behavior. Developers are also encouraged to implement safeguards such as unremovable watermarks and usage tracking to deter misuse.

3. Accuracy and verification of AI outputs

AI generated content isn't always reliable nor accurate. AI isn't perfect. Users should critically evaluate AI outputs and cross-check information with credible sources. Relying solely on AI without verification can lead to the spread of misinformation and errors. It is essential to understand the limitations of AI models, including potential biases in training data that can affect output quality. Encouraging a culture of skepticism and fact-checking can help mitigate risks associated with AI-generated content. Users should also be educated on how to identify and address inaccuracies in AI outputs.

4. Bias and Fairness

AI systems follow patterns in their training data, which can lead to biased outputs if the data itself is biased. It is important to recognize and address these biases to ensure fairness and equity in AI applications. Developers should strive to create diverse and representative training datasets, while users should be aware of potential biases in AI-generated content. Promoting transparency about AI decision-making processes can help build trust and accountability. Ongoing monitoring and evaluation of AI systems are necessary to identify and mitigate bias over time. Developers should be transparent about the limitations of their models and actively work to reduce bias in AI outputs.

5. Privacy and Accountability

The privacy of users are put at risk when using AI tools that collect and process personal data. It is essential to ensure that AI applications comply with data protection regulations and respect user privacy. Users should be informed about how their data is used and have control over their personal information. Accountability mechanisms should be in place to address any misuse or breaches of privacy. Developers must implement robust security measures to protect user data and maintain trust. Ethical AI use requires a commitment to safeguarding privacy and ensuring responsible data handling practices.
