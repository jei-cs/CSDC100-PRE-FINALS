Frequently Asked Questions about GenAI

Everything you need to know about Generative AI, its capabilities, limitations, and ethical considerations.

Generative AI, GenAI for short, is artificial intelligence (AI) that can generate/create original text, image, video, audio, or content in response to a user’s prompt. It uses machine learning models trained on large datasets to understand patterns and produce new, similar content.

Generative AI relies on sophisticated machine learning models called deep learning models algorithms that simulate the learning and decision-making processes of the human brain. These models work by identifying and encoding the patterns and relationships in huge amounts of data, and then using that information to understand users' natural language requests or questions and respond with relevant new content. For example, a Generative AI model trained on a large dataset of text can learn the structure and style of human language, allowing it to generate coherent and contextually relevant text based on a given prompt.

GenAI is a very powerful tool for efficiency. It can help users summarize very long texts, generate ideas, search the web for multiple answers (GPT-5, Gemini), and a whole lot more. Any laborious tasks can be made 100x more efficient and automated using GenAI given the right instructions

Some notable GenAI tools that are widely known and used today are ChatGPT, Gemini, and Grok from X. These tools allow you to generate content based on your prompt whether it may be text, image, or videos.

Despite its impressive capabilities, Generative AI has several limitations and risks. It can sometimes produce incorrect or nonsensical information, known as "hallucinations." Additionally, GenAI models may inadvertently generate biased or harmful content if they are trained on biased datasets. There are also concerns about privacy, as these models may inadvertently reveal sensitive information from their training data. Furthermore, the misuse of Generative AI for malicious purposes, such as creating deepfakes or spreading misinformation, poses significant ethical and societal challenges.

It is generally not advisable to share sensitive personal information with Generative AI tools, as there are potential privacy and security risks. While many GenAI platforms implement measures to protect user data, there is always a risk of data breaches or misuse of information. Users are advised to proceed with caution and avoid sharing confidential data to safeguard privacy.

It is highly unlikely AI will completely "replace" jobs or careers. However, AI will definitely change and revolutionize the way we work. Some tasks, mostly those that are repetitive and laborious in nature, may be automated using AI. This does not mean that jobs will be lost or you will get replaced, rather, AI will be used as a tool to improve human productivity and efficiency. Embracing AI as a complementary tool rather than a threat can lead to new opportunities for innovation and growth in various industries.
